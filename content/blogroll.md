Are you new to the web, and is your experience limited to apps and algorithms feeding you "content" (a term that carries a certain irony in my native Romanian language)? 

I recommend reading the following articles to understand the "alternative" ways you can consume information, rather than being consumed by it:

1.  **[Why Use RSS in 2026?](https://rss.app/guides/what-is-rss/why-use-rss)**
    -  An exploration of why decentralized syndication remains the most efficient way to stay informed.
2.  **[Revisiting RSS](https://davepeck.org/2018/08/16/revisiting-rss/)** 
    - A look back at the protocol's history and its necessity for a healthier digital diet.
3.  **[How Google helped destroy adoption of RSS feeds](https://openrss.org/blog/how-google-helped-destroy-adoption-of-rss-feeds)** 
    - A breakdown of how corporate interests shifted the web away from user-controlled feeds.
4.  **[Where have all the websites gone?](https://www.fromjason.xyz/p/notebook/where-have-all-the-websites-gone/)**
    - A thoughtful piece on the decline of the independent web and the rise of platform silos.
5.  **[IndieWeb principles and I](https://hamatti.org/posts/indieweb-principles-and-i/)**
    - An introduction to the philosophy of owning your data and your digital identity.
