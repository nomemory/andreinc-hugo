<div class="mp" id="p00041">
        <p><a class="mpl" href="/olympiad/p00041/">Problem 00041</a></p> 
        <p>Let \(k, x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n \in (0,1)\) such that \(k \gt \max\{x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n\}\). Prove the following inequality:</p>
        <p class="mpc">
            \[
                \sqrt{\prod_{i=1}^n x_i} + \sqrt{\prod_{i=1}^n(k-x_i)} \lt k
            \]
        </p>
        <details>
            <summary>Hint 1</summary>
            <p>If \(a\in (0,1)\) then \(\sqrt{a}\lt\sqrt[3]{a}\).</p>
        </details>
        <details>
            <summary>Solution</summary>
            <p>Since \(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n \in (0,1)\) and \(k \in (0,1)\) then \(\prod x_i \in (0,1)\) and \(\prod (k-x_i) \in (0,1)\).</p>
            <p>Taking this into consideration:</p>
            <p class="mpc">
                \[
                    \begin{align}
                    \sqrt{\prod_{i=1}^n x_i} \leq \sqrt[n]{\prod_{i=1}^n x_i} \overbrace{\leq}^{AM-GM} \frac{1}{n} \cdot \sum_{i=1}^n x_i \tag{1}
                    \end{align}
                \]
            </p>
            <p>In the same time, by applying the AM-GM inequality:</p>
            <p class="mpc">
                \[
                    \begin{align}
                     \sqrt{\prod_{i=1}^n(k-x_i)} \leq \sqrt[n]{\prod_{i=1}^n(k-x_i)} \overbrace{\leq}^{AM-GM} \frac{1}{n}\cdot \sum_{i=1}^n (k-x_i) \tag{2}
                    \end{align}
                \]
            </p>
            <p>After summing \((1)\) and \((2)\), our initial inequality is proven:</p>
            <p class="mpc">
                \[
                    \sqrt{\prod_{i=1}^n x_i} +  \sqrt{\prod_{i=1}^n(k-x_i)} \leq \frac{1}{n}\cdot \sum_{i=1}^n x_i + \frac{1}{n}\cdot \sum_{i=1}^n (k-x_i) \Leftrightarrow
                \]
                \[
                    \sqrt{\prod_{i=1}^n x_i} +  \sqrt{\prod_{i=1}^n(k-x_i)} \leq \frac{1}{n}\cdot \sum_{i=1}^n x_i + k - \frac{1}{n}\cdot \sum_{i=1}^n x_i = k
                \]
            </p>
        </details>
        <details>
            <summary>Source</summary>

        </details>
