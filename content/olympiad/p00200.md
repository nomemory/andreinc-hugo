<p><a class="mpl" href="/olympiad/p00200/">Problem 00200</a></p>
<p>If \(a, b, c \in (0,1)\) or \(a,b,c \in (1, \infty)\), prove that:</p>
<p>
    \[
        \log_a b + \log_b c + \log_c a + \frac{3}{\log_b a + \log_c b + \log_a c} \geq 4
    \]
</p>
<details>
    <summary>Solution</summary>
    <p>Since \(a, b, c\) are all in \((0,1)\) or \((1, \infty)\), all our logarithms are positive numbers. We keep this in mind.</p>
    <p>To simplify the problem, we can perform the following substitution:</p>
    <p class="mpc">
        \[
            x = \log_a b, \quad y = \log_b c, \quad z = \log_c a
        \]
    </p>
    <p>We also observe that:</p>
    <p class="mpc">
        \[
            \log_a b \cdot \log_b c \cdot \log_c a = 1 \implies x \cdot y \cdot z = 1
        \]
    </p>
    <p>At the same time, because of the AM-GM inequality:</p>
    <p class="mpc">
        \[
            S = x + y + z \geq 3 \sqrt[3]{xyz} = 3(1) = 3
        \]
    </p>
    <p>The inequality we have to prove is:</p>
    <p class="mpc">
        \[
            x + y + z + \frac{3}{\frac{1}{x}+\frac{1}{y}+\frac{1}{z}} \geq 4 \iff S + \frac{3}{xy+yz+zx} \geq 4
        \]
    </p>
    <p>But because \(\frac{S^2}{3} \geq xy+yz+zx \), we should try proving:</p>
    <p>
        \[
            S + \frac{3}{xy+yz+zx} \geq S + \frac{3}{\frac{S^2}{3}} = S + \frac{9}{S^2} \stackrel{?}{\geq} 4
        \]
    </p>
    <p>So if we manage to prove that \(S + \frac{9}{S^2} \geq 4\), our inequality is proven. This is easier than expected:</p>
    <p>
        \[
            S + \frac{9}{S^2} \geq 4 \iff S^3-4S^2+9 = (S-3)(S^2-S-3) \geq 0
        \]
    </p>
    <p>The last inequality is true for every \(S \geq 3\), completing the proof.</p>
</details>
